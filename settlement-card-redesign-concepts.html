Settlement Card Redesign: Design Concepts

6 approaches to integrating practice area tags and law firm names

Problem Statement

The current settlement cards display case type, settlement amount, AI-generated description, and optional matching criteria badges. We need to add two new data points without compromising the existing information hierarchy:

  • Practice area classification: Category tag (e.g., "Motor Vehicle Accident", "Slip & Fall", "Workplace Injury")
  • Law firm name: Attribution to the firm that handled the case (e.g., "Smith & Associates", "Johnson Law Firm")

Context & Constraints

  • Cards appear in a responsive grid: 3 columns (desktop), 2 columns (tablet), 1 column (mobile)
  • Settlement amount is the primary data point and must remain highly visible
  • Current design uses: white background, 16px border radius, 20px padding, shadow on hover
  • Existing color scheme: gradient blue for amounts, green for matching badges, black/gray for text
  • Must maintain professional, trustworthy appearance
  • Accessibility compliance required (WCAG AA contrast ratios)
Concept 1: Top Badge + Subtle Attribution

Places the practice area as a prominent badge at the very top of the card, establishing category context immediately. The law firm appears below the title as a subtle attribution line with an icon. This approach prioritizes practice area visibility while keeping law firm information present but secondary.

Pros

  • Practice area is immediately visible, enabling quick scanning
  • Badge creates strong visual category system for filtering/recognition
  • Law firm doesn't compete with primary information
  • Clean, uncluttered design maintains current aesthetic

Cons

  • Law firm might be too subtle for firms wanting visibility
  • Adds vertical height to card
  • Badge could feel redundant if case title already indicates type

Concept 2: Header Integration - Dual Meta Row

Integrates both new fields into a compact meta row above the main title and amount. Practice area appears as an uppercase label on the left, law firm on the right. This creates a clear header structure with three tiers: meta info, primary info, details.

Pros

  • Both fields receive equal visual weight
  • Compact layout doesn't add much vertical space
  • Creates clear information hierarchy with distinct zones
  • Law firm gets better visibility than Concept 1

Cons

  • Could feel cluttered on narrow mobile screens
  • Meta row might compete with title for attention
  • Requires careful text truncation on smaller screens

Concept 3: Sidebar Accent + Footer Attribution

Uses a colored left-border accent (changes per practice area) with a small practice area label at the top. Law firm appears in a subtle footer section at the bottom, separated by a divider. This approach uses color and position to organize information without badges.

Pros

  • Color-coded sidebar enables instant category recognition
  • Unique visual treatment stands out from other concepts
  • Footer placement for law firm feels like natural attribution
  • Maintains clean central content area

Cons

  • Requires color system definition and maintenance
  • Law firm visibility is lowest of all concepts
  • Footer might be missed during quick scanning

Concept 4: Bottom Meta Bar - Equal Prominence

Preserves the original header completely unchanged and adds both new fields in a footer meta bar below the AI overview. Practice area appears as a chip/pill on the left, law firm as text on the right. This minimizes disruption to the existing design.

Pros

  • Zero changes to existing header - lowest implementation risk
  • Both fields clearly visible in dedicated space
  • Easy to scan footer meta bar for additional details
  • Practice chip creates visual interest at bottom

Cons

  • Information is furthest from settlement amount
  • May be overlooked if users focus on header/amount
  • Adds card height more than top-placement concepts

Concept 5: Icon-Based Tag System

Both practice area and law firm appear as colorful, icon-enhanced tags at the top of the card. Each tag has a distinct gradient background and icon, creating a badge-like system that's visually engaging and easy to scan. This is the most vibrant approach.

Pros

  • Most visually distinctive and engaging design
  • Icons aid quick recognition and add personality
  • Color differentiation makes both fields stand out
  • Tag system feels modern and user-friendly

Cons

  • Could feel too colorful/busy for professional context
  • Requires icon library for practice areas and law firms
  • Takes up most horizontal space at top of card

Concept 6: Compact Inline Metadata

Integrates practice area and law firm as inline metadata directly beneath the settlement title, separated by bullet points. This creates a compact, text-based solution that maintains maximum information density without adding separate UI elements. Most conservative approach.

Pros

  • Most space-efficient solution - minimal vertical growth
  • Natural reading flow from title to metadata
  • Simple, professional appearance
  • Easiest to implement - pure text solution

Cons

  • Less visually distinctive - harder to scan
  • Practice area doesn't stand out for categorization
  • Could get lost in the text hierarchy

Design Recommendation

Primary Recommendation: Concept 2 (Header Integration - Dual Meta Row)

After analyzing all six concepts, Concept 2 provides the best balance of visibility, hierarchy, and usability for the following reasons:

  • Balanced visibility: Both practice area and law firm receive appropriate prominence without overwhelming the settlement amount
  • Clear hierarchy: Three-tier structure (meta → primary → details) creates intuitive scanning pattern
  • Space efficiency: Compact meta row adds minimal height while maintaining readability
  • Scannability: Practice area positioned at top-left enables quick category recognition across the grid
  • Professional appearance: Text-based solution maintains trustworthy, clean aesthetic
  • Responsive friendly: Can gracefully stack or truncate on mobile with clear fallback patterns

Alternative for Consideration: Concept 1 (Top Badge + Subtle Attribution)

If practice area categorization needs stronger visual emphasis (e.g., for filtering UI or color-coding), Concept 1 would be the stronger choice. The badge treatment makes categories immediately recognizable and creates better visual grouping in the grid.

Implementation Notes:

  • Ensure practice area text truncates with ellipsis beyond ~20 characters
  • Law firm name should truncate at ~25 characters with full name in hover tooltip
  • On mobile (<640px), consider stacking the meta row or showing only practice area with law firm in collapsed state
  • Test color contrast for practice area text to ensure WCAG AA compliance (4.5:1 minimum)
  • Consider adding subtle hover effects that highlight the meta row for enhanced interactivity
