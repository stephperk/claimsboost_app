Firm Highlights Design Concepts

6 Different Approaches to Displaying Law Firm Highlights

Design Challenge

Redesign the "Firm Highlights" section on law firm profile pages to better showcase factual information about the firm in a scannable, professional, and trustworthy manner.

Current State

  • Simple vertical list with blue checkmark icons
  • 5 bullet points with facts like "Combined legal experience of over 100 years"
  • Header reads "FIRM HIGHLIGHTS" with a stars icon
  • Minimal visual hierarchy and differentiation

Requirements

  • Present factual highlights about the law firm
  • Be scannable and easy to read
  • Feel professional and trustworthy
  • Work well on both desktop and mobile
  • Integrate with existing design system (white cards, blue gradient accents)

Concept 1: Enhanced Classic List

Refine the existing list approach with improved hover states, subtle backgrounds, and better spacing for enhanced scannability while maintaining familiarity.

✓ Pros

  • Familiar pattern that users already understand
  • Minimal implementation effort - incremental improvement
  • Works well with long-form text descriptions
  • Excellent mobile compatibility with simple vertical stacking

✗ Cons

  • Limited visual differentiation from current design
  • Doesn't emphasize key numbers or statistics effectively
  • May feel too conservative or dated

Concept 2: Stats-Focused Grid

Transform highlights into distinct stat cards that emphasize key numbers and achievements. Each card features an icon, prominent statistic, and supporting description.

✓ Pros

  • Strong visual hierarchy that draws eye to key numbers
  • Cards create clear separation between different highlights
  • Hover effects add interactivity and modern feel
  • Grid layout makes efficient use of space

✗ Cons

  • Requires restructuring content to fit card format
  • Less effective for highlights without clear numeric data
  • May require more vertical space on mobile

Concept 3: Timeline Journey

Present highlights as a visual journey using a timeline format with connected dots. Creates a narrative flow that guides the eye through each achievement.

✓ Pros

  • Creates visual narrative and flow between highlights
  • Timeline metaphor suggests growth and progression
  • Unique approach that differentiates from competitors
  • Gradient line adds visual interest without clutter

✗ Cons

  • Timeline implies chronological order which may not apply
  • Left padding reduces content width
  • May be confusing if highlights aren't sequential achievements

Concept 4: Icon-Led Feature Cards

Transform highlights into compact feature cards with prominent circular icon badges. Creates a modern, app-like interface that emphasizes visual symbols.

✓ Pros

  • Strong visual icons make content scannable at a glance
  • Circular badges create focal points and visual rhythm
  • Card-based design feels modern and premium
  • Hover animations add polish and interactivity

✗ Cons

  • Limited text space may require condensed descriptions
  • Three-column grid may be cramped on smaller screens
  • Icons may not clearly represent all types of highlights

Concept 5: Numbered Priority List

Use sequential numbering with alternating backgrounds to create hierarchy and suggest priority. Numbers in gradient circles provide clear visual anchors.

✓ Pros

  • Numbers provide clear structure and ordering
  • Alternating backgrounds improve scannability
  • Works well with longer text descriptions
  • Gradient number badges add visual interest

✗ Cons

  • Numbering implies ranking which may not be intended
  • Requires deciding on a specific order of importance
  • May feel too structured or rigid

Concept 6: Compact Achievement Pills

Display highlights as compact, tag-like pills that can wrap naturally. Emphasizes conciseness and creates a cloud of achievements that's easy to scan.

✓ Pros

  • Extremely space-efficient and scannable
  • Natural wrapping works well on all screen sizes
  • Modern, badge-like aesthetic
  • Encourages concise, impactful messaging

✗ Cons

  • Requires condensing content to short phrases
  • Less suitable for detailed explanations
  • May lose context or nuance with brevity

Recommendation: Hybrid Approach

After analyzing all six concepts, I recommend a hybrid approach combining Concept 2 (Stats-Focused Grid) with elements of Concept 1 (Enhanced Classic List).

Recommended Strategy

  • Use the stats grid (Concept 2) when highlights contain numeric data - This makes key achievements like "$500M Recovered" and "100+ Years" immediately scannable and impactful
  • Fall back to enhanced list format (Concept 1) for non-numeric highlights - Awards, policies, and availability information work better in sentence form
  • Combine both in a single section - Display 2-4 stat cards at the top, followed by a list of additional highlights below
  • Maintain consistent styling - Use the same blue gradient, card borders, and spacing across both formats

Why This Works Best

  • Maximum impact: Numbers get the visual emphasis they deserve while preserving space for detailed information
  • Flexibility: Works whether a firm has many stats or mostly qualitative achievements
  • Scannability: Users can quickly grasp key numbers, then dive deeper into additional facts
  • Mobile-friendly: Grid collapses to single column, list remains scannable
  • Professional yet modern: Balances innovation with trustworthiness

Alternative Recommendation

If you prefer a single unified approach, Concept 5 (Numbered Priority List) offers the best balance of:

  • Clear visual hierarchy with numbered badges
  • Good support for both stats and descriptive text
  • Strong scannability with alternating backgrounds
  • Professional appearance that builds trust
