Settlement Card Category Labels - Minimalist Concepts

Design Brief

Goal: Create subtle, professional category indicators that don't compete with the settlement amount.

  • Minimalistic color palette using muted tones
  • No bold colored borders (except $1M+ gold edge)
  • Must work in 3-column grid layout
  • Professional, clean aesthetic

Concept 1: File Folder Tabs (Offset)

Classic file folder approach with tabs positioned at the top-left, creating a familiar organizational metaphor.

Pros:

  • Immediately recognizable filing system metaphor
  • Minimal visual interference with card content
  • Each category has distinct subtle tint
  • Easy to scan across multiple cards

Cons:

  • Tab positioning may feel asymmetric
  • Requires extra top margin spacing
  • Category text needs to be quite small

Concept 2: Hanging File Center Tab

Centered pill-shaped tabs that appear to "hang" from the top of the card, inspired by hanging file folders.

Pros:

  • Perfect symmetry creates visual balance
  • Distinctive, elegant appearance
  • Category immediately visible but not dominant
  • Clean, modern interpretation of filing system

Cons:

  • Center positioning may interfere with card title
  • Requires careful spacing considerations
  • May feel too decorative for some contexts

Concept 3: Corner Paper Tag

Small tag in the top-right corner with a folded paper effect, like a price tag or product label.

Pros:

  • Extremely subtle and non-intrusive
  • Unique folded corner adds tactile quality
  • Positioned away from main content
  • Easy to implement with minimal space usage

Cons:

  • May be too subtle for quick scanning
  • Corner position feels secondary
  • Small size limits text legibility

Concept 4: Subtle Full-Width Header Bar

Full-width header strip with minimal tint that blends seamlessly with the card's top border radius.

Pros:

  • Clear visual hierarchy from top to bottom
  • Category is immediately visible
  • Feels integrated with card structure
  • Professional, polished appearance

Cons:

  • Takes up more vertical space
  • May compete slightly more with content
  • Less unique compared to tab concepts

Concept 5: Floating Badge

Small, pill-shaped badge floating in the top-right corner with subtle shadow for depth.

  • Ultra-minimal footprint
  • Modern, clean aesthetic
  • Doesn't interfere with any card content
  • Subtle shadow adds professional polish

Cons:

  • Very small and may be overlooked
  • Limited space for category text
  • Position might conflict with other UI elements

Recommendation Summary

All six concepts successfully achieve the minimalist, professional aesthetic while maintaining clear category distinction. Key considerations:

  • Most Traditional: Concepts 1 (File Folder) and 4 (Full-Width Header) offer familiar patterns
  • Most Elegant: Concepts 2 (Hanging Tab) and 5 (Floating Badge) provide sophisticated, modern looks
  • Most Unique: Concepts 3 (Corner Tag) and 6 (Side Tab) offer distinctive approaches
  • Best Readability: Concepts 1, 2, and 4 with horizontal text orientation
  • Least Intrusive: Concepts 5 and 3 with minimal visual weight

All concepts maintain: 3-column grid compatibility, gold edge for $1M+ settlements, and professional color palette.
