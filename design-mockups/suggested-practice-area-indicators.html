Secondary Practice Area Indicator

Design Challenge

We need a visual indicator for "related/suggested" practice areas - ones that are semantically similar to the user's search but not exact matches. This should be visually distinct from both the gray (no match) and green filled (exact match) pills, while clearly communicating "this could be relevant to your search."

Gray = No match to search
✓ Green Filled = Exact match (score >= 0.8)
Green Outline = Related match (NEW)

Recommendation

Option 2 (Light Green Fill) creates the most natural visual hierarchy: the filled green pill clearly says "yes, this matches," the light green tint says "this is related," and gray says "no connection." It's intuitive and doesn't add visual noise.

Alternative: If you want something more prominent, Option 1 (Solid Green Outline) is clean and professional. The outline style clearly differentiates from both the filled and gray states.
